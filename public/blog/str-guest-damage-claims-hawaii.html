How to Handle STR Guest Damage Claims in Hawaii (Step-by-Step)

Prevention: The Documentation Protocol That Makes Claims Winnable

The outcome of a damage claim is almost entirely determined before the guest ever checks in. Hosts who win disputes consistently do one thing differently: they document everything, every time, with timestamps.

This isn't about distrust. It's about having evidence that establishes a clear baseline — so that when something changes, the change is provable.

The Pre-Stay Documentation Standard

Within 24 hours before each guest arrival, complete the following:

Photo Documentation (30+ Photos Minimum)

  • Every room: Wide shot showing full room, close-up of any existing wear or marks
  • All surfaces: Countertops, floors, walls, ceilings (check for any staining or damage)
  • All appliances: Refrigerator interior, oven interior, microwave, dishwasher
  • All furniture: Each sofa (front and sides), dining chairs, beds (top and sides)
  • Outdoor areas: Lanai, pool deck, outdoor furniture, BBQ condition
  • Entry and doors: Front door, all sliding doors, lock hardware
  • Valuables inventory: Any items of value in the property — art, electronics, decorative items

Critical: Every photo must be timestamped. Use your phone's native camera app (which embeds metadata), or use a property management app like Properly, Breezeway, or HappyCo that timestamps and stores inspection photos automatically.

Video Walkthrough

In addition to photos, record a 3–5 minute narrated video walkthrough of the entire property. Walk into every room, narrate what you see, open every appliance. This is harder to dispute than individual photos because it shows continuity — no one can claim a photo was taken at a different time or staged.

Document Existing Issues

Note every existing imperfection in a pre-stay report: the small chip in the bathroom tile, the scuff on the baseboard, the sliding door that needs a firm push to latch. These pre-existing issues are ammunition for guests who want to dispute your claim by pointing to damage that was already there. Document them first.

Check-In Guest Acknowledgment

For direct bookings and VRBO reservations where you have guest contact, send a pre-arrival message that includes: "We've completed our pre-arrival inspection — photos and condition notes are on file. If you notice any damage or issues on arrival that aren't mentioned in the property listing, please notify us within 2 hours of check-in."

This sets expectations, discourages abuse, and creates a record that you invited guests to flag pre-existing issues — which they didn't.

Post-Checkout: The Critical 24-Hour Window

The window between one guest checking out and the next checking in is when damage claims live or die. Speed matters because:

  • Airbnb requires claims to be submitted before the next guest's check-in
  • Damage evidence degrades — what's clearly fresh on day one may look ambiguous by day three
  • Platform algorithms favor claims submitted with same-day documentation

Post-Checkout Inspection Protocol

Conduct your post-stay inspection as soon as possible after checkout, ideally within 2 hours.

  1. Walk the full property systematically — same route as your pre-stay inspection. Don't rush to assess damage first; walk the whole property first to get the full picture.
  2. Photograph everything — even if nothing appears damaged. Document the post-stay condition as thoroughly as the pre-stay condition. You want a complete before/after record.
  3. Identify discrepancies — compare mentally (or with your documentation) to the pre-stay photos. Note anything that has changed.
  4. Photograph damage in context — show the damaged item in the room, then close-up detail. Both views are important.
  5. Document cleaning issues separately — excessive mess beyond normal checkout cleaning (full trashcans in every room, pet evidence, leftover food) may qualify for extra cleaning fees, which are separate from damage claims.

O'ahu-Specific Post-Stay Issues to Check

Hawaii vacation rentals have some property-specific damage patterns mainland owners may not anticipate:

  • Sunscreen staining: Hawaii-strength sunscreen permanently stains upholstery, towels, and mattress covers. Document any new staining immediately.
  • Sand and salt damage: Beachfront and Kailua/Lanikai properties see guests tracking beach sand into the property — normal up to a point, but excessive sand damage to flooring or clogged drains can exceed normal wear.
  • Pool/spa misuse: Glass in pool areas (prohibited by most pool permits), chemical imbalance from guests adding their own chemicals, and equipment abuse are common at pool properties.
  • AC damage: Guests leaving doors or windows open while running AC is common and strains systems. Note if the system sounds different after checkout.
  • Lanai furniture rearrangement and damage: Outdoor furniture dragged across lanai surfaces causes scratching. Document the furniture arrangement and surface condition pre and post stay.

Filing an Airbnb AirCover Claim — Step by Step

Airbnb AirCover for Hosts covers up to $3 million in guest-caused damage. Understanding its limitations is as important as knowing how to file.

What AirCover Covers

  • Damage to the property and its contents caused directly by guests
  • Deep cleaning costs beyond normal checkout cleaning (e.g., biohazard cleanup, pet odor remediation)
  • Lost income during repair period (subject to review)
  • Pet damage (if pets were not permitted)

What AirCover Does NOT Cover

  • Pre-existing damage (this is why pre-stay documentation matters — without it, Airbnb may rule all damage as pre-existing)
  • Normal wear and tear
  • Cash, securities, or personal valuables
  • Items damaged by weather or natural events
  • Vehicles
  • Damage not reported within the claim window

The Filing Process

  1. Go to airbnb.com/resolutions immediately after documenting damage
  2. Select the reservation and choose "Request money" or "Report damage"
  3. Submit all documentation:
    • Pre-stay photos/video (timestamped)
    • Post-stay photos/video (timestamped)
    • Description of damage — factual, specific, no emotional language
    • Repair or replacement quotes from licensed Hawaii contractors (required for claims above $500)
    • Receipts if you've already had repairs done
  4. Give the guest 24 hours to respond — Airbnb requires this before escalating to AirCover
  5. If the guest declines or doesn't respond: escalate to AirCover in the Resolution Center
  6. Airbnb's team reviews within 5–7 business days and issues a determination

Getting Repair Quotes in Hawaii

For claims above $500, Airbnb requires documentation of actual repair costs. On O'ahu, getting contractor quotes takes time — trades are in high demand. If you're pursuing a significant claim:

  • Get a minimum of 2 quotes from licensed contractors (verify at pvl.ehawaii.gov)
  • If you can't get quotes before the claim deadline, submit a placeholder quote noting "formal quotes pending" and upload them as soon as available
  • For furniture or fixture replacement, provide manufacturer pricing or comparable item listings on Amazon/local suppliers

The #1 reason Airbnb AirCover claims are denied: Insufficient pre-stay documentation. If you can't prove the damage wasn't there before the guest arrived, Airbnb defaults to no payment. This is not negotiable — their system is built on documentation.

Filing a VRBO Damage Claim

VRBO gives hosts two options for damage protection: a refundable security deposit or a non-refundable damage protection fee. Each has a different claims process.

Option A: Security Deposit (Host Preference)

If you've set a security deposit on your VRBO listing:

  1. Document damage thoroughly within 24 hours of checkout
  2. Submit a damage claim through the VRBO Host Dashboard within 7 days of checkout
  3. VRBO reviews documentation and, if the claim is valid, releases deposit funds to the host
  4. The guest is notified and has the opportunity to dispute
  5. If disputed, VRBO mediates — your documentation quality is decisive

Option B: Damage Protection Fee (Guest-Paid Insurance)

If the guest purchased VRBO's damage protection (through CSA/Generali):

  1. Document damage and file through VRBO's Resolution Center
  2. The claim is routed to the insurance provider
  3. You'll receive a claim number and communicate directly with the insurer
  4. Submit all documentation to the insurer (not VRBO) for processing
  5. Processing typically takes 2–4 weeks

VRBO vs. Airbnb claims process: VRBO's system generally gives hosts more direct access to security deposits than Airbnb's AirCover. However, VRBO's maximum deposit protection and the documentation requirements are similar. The same pre-stay documentation protocol protects you on both platforms.

Direct Booking Security Deposit Claims

For properties booked directly (through your own website or direct inquiry), the damage recovery process is entirely in your hands — which means your rental agreement, deposit collection method, and documentation are the only protection you have.

Direct Booking Security Deposit Best Practices

  • Security deposit amount: For O'ahu vacation rentals, $500–$2,500 is standard depending on property value and rental rate. High-value estates ($5M+) typically require $2,000–$5,000.
  • Collection method: Credit card authorization hold is the most common. Stripe, Square, and Authorize.net all support security deposit holds. The hold is released after checkout if no damage is claimed.
  • Rental agreement language: Your agreement must specify: what constitutes damage vs. normal wear, the timeline for claiming (typically 48–72 hours post checkout), the documentation standard you'll use, and the guest's right to dispute.
  • Dispute process without a platform: If a guest disputes your direct booking damage claim and you've charged their card, they may file a credit card chargeback. Your evidence to the card issuer (Stripe, your bank) is the same documentation you'd submit to Airbnb — pre-stay and post-stay photos, repair quotes, rental agreement.

Small Claims Court for Unresolved Direct Booking Disputes

For O'ahu properties, unresolved direct booking damage disputes above your security deposit can be filed in Hawaii Small Claims Court (First Circuit, Honolulu). The limit is $10,000. You don't need an attorney. The process takes 2–4 months but is relatively straightforward with good documentation. Filing fee is approximately $30–$55 depending on claim amount.

When Guests Dispute Your Claim

Guest disputes are common even when damage is clear. The most common strategies guests use:

  • "That damage was already there when we arrived"
  • "Normal wear and tear" (applied to clear damage)
  • "We only had [X] guests — we couldn't have caused that"
  • Counter-claiming that something was wrong with the property

How to Respond to a Dispute

  1. Do not get emotional. Every word you write in a platform dispute is read by a human reviewer. Hosts who sound frustrated or accusatory lose credibility. Stay factual, specific, and professional.
  2. Let your documentation do the talking. "Our pre-stay inspection on [date] at [time] shows the sofa in perfect condition. Our post-stay inspection [hours later] documents the stain. Timestamped photos of both are attached."
  3. Address their specific claim. If they say "that damage was already there" — show your pre-stay photo with the timestamp proving it wasn't. Don't just resubmit your full documentation; respond specifically to their argument.
  4. Escalate if needed. If the platform's initial reviewer rules against you unfairly: request a supervisor review, cite specific documentation they appear to have overlooked, and reference the platform's own policies.

When to Accept a Partial Resolution

Sometimes accepting a partial settlement is the right call — especially for smaller claims where the time cost of fighting outweighs the disputed amount. A useful heuristic: if pursuing the full claim requires more than 3 hours of your time and the disputed amount is under $300, the partial resolution is often the better economic choice.

Hawaii STR Insurance: Your Real Last Line of Defense

AirCover and VRBO damage protection are supplemental coverage mechanisms, not insurance. Your actual last line of defense — for significant damage, liability claims, or losses the platforms won't cover — is a dedicated short-term rental insurance policy.

Standard Homeowner's Insurance Doesn't Cover You

This is the most common and costly misconception among O'ahu vacation rental owners. Standard homeowner's policies explicitly exclude losses occurring during commercial rental activity. If a guest slips by your pool and sues you, or a guest starts a fire that damages your $4M Kahala property, your homeowner's policy will likely deny the claim entirely if the property was being operated as a vacation rental at the time of loss.

Hawaii STR Insurance Options

  • Proper Insurance: Purpose-built STR policy. Covers commercial liability, guest damage, loss of rental income, and medical payments to guests. Available in Hawaii. Typically $2,000–$5,000/year for an O'ahu vacation rental depending on property value and revenue.
  • CBIZ Vacation Rental Insurance: Competitive for higher-value properties. Good coverage for furniture and contents damage specifically.
  • Foremost/Farmers Vacation Rental Policy: Available through local Hawaii agents. Generally more affordable but with lower coverage limits.
  • Lloyds of London through a Hawaii specialty broker: For ultra-high-value properties ($5M+), a Lloyd's policy through a specialty broker provides the broadest coverage available.

O'ahu property owners should also verify that their STR insurance satisfies the Honolulu DPP's requirements for legally operating a short-term rental — some permit types require proof of liability coverage as a condition of the permit.

Frequently Asked Questions

Does Airbnb AirCover cover guest damage to my O'ahu vacation rental?

Airbnb AirCover provides up to $3 million in damage protection for eligible guest-caused damage, but has significant exclusions: pre-existing damage, normal wear and tear, cash and valuables, and damage not documented within the claim window. AirCover supplements but does not replace proper documentation and dedicated STR insurance. Most experienced O'ahu vacation rental managers carry both.

What documentation do I need before guests check in to support a damage claim?

Timestamped photos of every room (30+ minimum), a narrated video walkthrough, documentation of all pre-existing issues, and appliance condition verification — all completed within 24 hours before arrival. Without solid pre-stay documentation, most platform damage claims will be denied.

How long do I have to file a damage claim on Airbnb?

You must submit within 14 days of checkout or before the next guest checks in — whichever comes first. For back-to-back bookings, this can be a window of hours. Same-day post-checkout inspections are non-negotiable for high-turnover O'ahu vacation rentals.

What should I do if a guest disputes my damage claim?

Stay factual and professional. Respond specifically to their argument using timestamped documentation. Escalate to platform supervisor review if the initial ruling is unfair. For unresolved direct booking disputes above your deposit, Hawaii Small Claims Court (First Circuit, Honolulu) handles claims up to $10,000 — no attorney needed, filing fee ~$30–$55.

Does my homeowner's insurance cover vacation rental guest damage in Hawaii?

Almost certainly not. Standard homeowner's policies exclude losses during commercial rental activity. Operating an STR without dedicated vacation rental insurance on O'ahu is a serious liability exposure. Proper Insurance, CBIZ, and Foremost/Farmers all offer Hawaii-compatible STR policies. Expect $2,000–$5,000/year for typical O'ahu vacation rental properties.
